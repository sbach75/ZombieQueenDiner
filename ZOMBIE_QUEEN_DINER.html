OUR STORY

Established in the late 1920s, the Queen Diner began as a beacon of classic American dining. Founded by Agnes O'Sullivan, our diner quickly became a beloved fixture in the heart of Queensville. Agnes's culinary talents were legendary, and her dishes were an embodiment of comfort and flavor. The Queen Diner was more than just a place to eat; it was a slice of life, offering hearty hamburgers, hand-cut fries, milkshakes, and homemade pies that could make anyone feel at home.

The world turned upside down in the year 1947 when the Great Apocalypse descended upon humanity. A cataclysmic event that shook the very foundations of our existence, it left in its wake a transformed world, inhabited by the undead.

In the aftermath of the Apocalypse, the Queen Diner underwent a profound transformation. Formerly human patrons turned into zombies, giving birth to a new era for our beloved diner. The Zombie Queen era had begun. While our appearance may have become a touch more macabre, the essence of the Zombie Queen Diner remains unaltered to the former Queen Diner.

The Zombie Queen Diner isn't just a place to eat; it's a testament to the enduring power of food, community, and adaptation. It's a place where the past and the present collide, where the undead come together to celebrate their shared love for a good meal. In this remarkable corner of our post-apocalyptic world, we continue to serve up iconic diner flavors, proving that even in the most extraordinary of circumstances, the spirit of a bygone era cannot be extinguished.
